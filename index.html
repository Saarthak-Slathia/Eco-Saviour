Water Conservation

"Water" is one of the most crucial components of life on Earth. The Earth is covered 75% with water, but out of this 75%, 99% of which is salt water or saline, that's not over yet the remaining 1% is fresh portable water to drink but, out of this 1%, 60% of fresh water is present in polar ice caps and glaciers, which we can't get as that is in the form of ice and it is very difficult to get as they are present in seas and oceans near the poles. But the remaining 30% is also not available easily, 29% of the remaining 30% water is ground water or present beneath the ground. The remaining 1% is available to us in the form of lakes, rivers, ponds etc. This 1% we use in our daily activities such as drinking, bathing, brushing, washing clothes etc. Also in other aspects we use water in water and amusement parks, swimming pools, medecines, factories etc. If we calculate that, the amount of portable, avaialable fresh water water available to us is 0.006%. Despit being a blue planet we face a shortage of water, incredibly shocking ! Clearly we need to save water.

Water Saving Tips

Here are some tips and tricks that you can use to save water:

  • Close the tap while brushing.
  • Use bucket instead of shower for bath.
  • Wash your car with bucket and not with pipe.
  • Encourage others to save water.
  • Fix leaking taps in your house.
  • Divert the waste water from the water filter and Air Conditioners to water the plants.
  • Close the tap tightly after use.

Air

Air is also one of the most crucial components of life. Air is the thing that we breath in and out and need to survive. A man can live for 3 days without food, almost 3 days without water, but only 3 minutes without air. So, what is the problem with air ? What can we do about it if something is there wrong with it ? In fact many things or mostly everything we have done have spoilt our atmosphere. Atmosphere is a blanket of air around the Earth that provides us with air, protects us from external space attacks example - Meteors etc. The atmosphere extends up to 1000km above the ground and is divided into 5 parts:

  • Troposphere
  • Stratosphere
  • Mesosphere
  • Thermosphere
  • Exosphere

Wind Energy

We can make better use of air as of water by generating electricity through air or moving wind. This can be achieved through windmills which convert wind energy to electrical energy. It is fan like thing which rotates when wind occurs and the rotating effect then is converted into electricity and then it is transfered to power houses.

Advantages of Windmills:

  • It is a green, eco-friendly source of generating electricity without polluting the air and the environment.
  • There is no carbon emissions associated with the working of wind turbines and windmills.
  • It reduces the demand for fossil fuels.

Disadvantages of Windmills:

  • Only one windmill won't satisfy, the amount of electricity geneerated through 1 windmill is very less.
  • Due to that, there needs to be a whole windmill power plant constructed which consumes a lot of land area.
  • Apart from the land area, the cost of installing 1 windmill is extremely high and a power plant would require emmensely large financial cost.
  • It is not a truly reliable source of energy or electricity as one cannot guarantee that if the wind will blow or not as wind is extremely important for windmills to work.

Water Pollution

Water Pollution is one of the major problems in our society. Water pollution refers to water being polluted by us and other means. We are polluting the water bodies like rivers, lakes, ponds, oceans, seas etc. which is very bad. The rivers Ganga, Yamuna, Mississippi are so much polluted with waste and garbage especially plastic etc. Here are the world's most polluted rivers list:

  • Mississippi River, U.S.A.
  • Sarno River, Italy

  • Jordan River, Israel

Tips and Techniques to save water:
